Flat Barbell Bench Press
3 sets × 8–10 reps · 60 sec rest
CHESTTRICEPS
Weight (kg)

How to perform
1
Lie flat, feet planted. Grip bar slightly wider than shoulder-width. Retract shoulder blades into the bench.
2
Lower slowly (2 sec) to mid-chest, elbows at ~45° from body.
3
Press powerfully back to start. Exhale on the way up.
Key tip: Don't let your lower back arch excessively - keep contact with the bench throughout the set.
02
Incline Dumbbell Press
2 sets × 10–12 reps · 45 sec rest
UPPER CHEST
Weight (kg)
Tap to add exercise photo
JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Set bench to 30° incline. Sit with dumbbells on knees, lie back. Hold at chest level, palms forward.
2
Press up and slightly inward. Lower slowly, feeling the upper chest stretch at the bottom.
Key tip: 30° hits the upper chest shelf - the area that makes the biggest visual difference as chest fat reduces.
03
Overhead Tricep Extension
2 sets × 12 reps · 45 sec rest
TRICEPS
Weight (kg)

JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Sit upright. Hold one dumbbell with both hands overhead, upper arms vertical close to head.
2
Bend elbows only - lower dumbbell behind head until forearms are parallel to floor. Extend back to start.
Key tip: Keep elbows pointing forward - don't let them flare wide during the movement.
04
Close-Grip Bench Press
2 sets × 10 reps · 60 sec rest
TRICEPSCHEST
Weight (kg)
Tap to add exercise photo
JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Same setup as bench press but hands shoulder-width. Elbows tucked close to sides throughout.
2
Lower to lower chest, press back explosively. Squeeze triceps hard at the top.
Key tip: Use lighter weight than regular bench - this is a finishing move, not a max effort exercise.

01
Standing DB Shoulder Press
3 sets × 10–12 reps · 60 sec rest
SHOULDERSCORE
Weight (kg)
Tap to add exercise photo
JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Stand feet shoulder-width, soft knees. Dumbbells at shoulder height, palms forward. Core braced.
2
Press overhead until arms nearly extended. Lower slowly. Keep strict - no leg drive.
Why standing works better: More natural shoulder path, reduces impingement risk, and engages core simultaneously.
02
Lateral Dumbbell Raise
2 sets × 15 reps · 45 sec rest
SIDE DELTS
Weight (kg)
Tap to add exercise photo
JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Stand with light dumbbells at sides, slight bend in elbows, slight lean forward from hips.
2
Raise both arms to sides leading with elbows. Stop at shoulder height. Lower slowly - 3 sec down.
Key tip: Use lighter weight than you think. Swinging to complete reps means too heavy.
03
Dumbbell Hammer Curl
2 sets × 12 reps · 45 sec rest
BRACHIALISFOREARMS
Weight (kg)
Tap to add exercise photo
JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Stand holding dumbbells at sides, thumbs pointing forward (neutral/hammer grip).
2
Curl one dumbbell up, thumb-up position throughout. Don't rotate the wrist. Lower fully then alternate.
Key tip: Builds the brachialis - under the bicep, creates arm thickness from all angles.
04
Concentration Curl
2 sets × 10 reps each arm · 45 sec rest
BICEP PEAKISOLATION
Weight (kg)
Tap to add exercise photo
JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Sit on bench, feet wide. Lean forward, rest back of upper arm against inner thigh. Arm fully extended.
2
Curl up in full arc. At top, rotate wrist so little finger is highest. Lower slowly and fully. Switch arms.
Why add this: Highest EMG activation for bicep peak. Pairs with hammer curls for full arm development - thickness AND height.
05
DB Skull Crusher
2 sets × 12 reps · 45 sec rest
TRICEPS
Weight (kg)
Tap to add exercise photo
JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Lie on bench, dumbbells above chest, arms vertical, palms facing each other.
2
Keeping upper arms still, bend elbows to lower dumbbells toward temples. Extend back. Only forearms move.
Key tip: Go lighter than expected. Fully isolates triceps - one of the best arm-building exercises.

01
Barbell Bent-Over Row
3 sets × 8–10 reps · 60 sec rest
UPPER BACKLATS
Weight (kg)
Tap to add exercise photo
JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Hinge forward at hips ~45°. Neutral spine. Overhand grip slightly wider than shoulders.
2
Pull bar to belly button, elbows driving back. Squeeze shoulder blades at top. Lower under control.
Key tip for back knots: Directly strengthens the muscles between your shoulder blades. Consistent rowing is the #1 fix for upper back stiffness.
02
Single-Arm DB Row
2 sets × 10 reps each side · 45 sec rest
LATSRHOMBOIDS
Weight (kg)
Tap to add exercise photo
JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Knee and same-side hand on bench, other foot on floor. Back flat and parallel to ground.
2
Pull dumbbell up to hip, elbow driving toward ceiling. Lower fully until arm is completely straight.
Key tip: Full range of motion at the bottom builds lat thickness and improves posture.
03
Dumbbell Bicep Curl
2 sets × 12 reps · 45 sec rest
BICEPS
Weight (kg)
Tap to add exercise photo
JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Stand, palms forward. Curl up rotating wrist at top so little finger is highest for max contraction.
2
Lower slowly - 2 full seconds - until arm is completely straight every rep.
Key tip: The slow lowering phase builds more muscle than the curl up. Don't skip it.
04
Bent-Over Rear Delt Raise
2 sets × 15 reps · 45 sec rest
REAR DELTSUPPER BACK
Weight (kg)
Tap to add exercise photo
JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Hinge forward, torso nearly parallel. Light dumbbells hanging, slight elbow bend.
2
Raise both arms to sides, squeezing rear delts and between shoulder blades at top. Lower slowly.
Critical for your goals: Directly targets muscles that knot from sitting. Will significantly reduce upper back stiffness over 4–6 weeks.

Plank Hold
3 sets × 25–40 sec · 30 sec rest
CORELOWER BACK

Tap to add exercise photo
JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Forearms on floor, elbows under shoulders, feet on toes. Lift hips to form a straight line. Brace abs, squeeze glutes.
2
Hold. Breathe normally. If hips drop noticeably, stop - quality over duration.
Lower back benefit: Builds deep stabilising muscles that support the lumbar spine - more effective for back pain than crunches.
02
Dead Bug
2 sets × 10 reps each side · 30 sec rest
DEEP CORELOWER BACK
Reps
Tap to add exercise photo
JPEG or PNG
How to perform
1
Lie on back, arms up to ceiling, knees bent 90° in the air. Press lower back into the floor throughout.
2
Slowly lower opposite arm and leg. Return. Switch sides. If back lifts off the floor - stop and reset.
Key tip: 3 sec out, 3 sec back. Retrains deep spinal stabilisers - essential for reducing lower back pain.

How to perform

Stand holding dumbbells at thighs, soft knee bend. Push hips backwards - let dumbbells slide down thighs.
2
Lower until strong hamstring stretch. Drive hips forward to stand. Squeeze glutes at top.
Lower back pain fix: Most important exercise for your lower back. Strengthens the entire posterior chain that supports your spine.
